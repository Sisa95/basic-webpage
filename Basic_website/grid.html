Historical places

Catle Of Goodhope
  • * It was built between 1666 and 1679 and is known as the oldest surviving building in South Africa.
    * The Castle formed part of a formidable defensive system at the Cape that discouraged attacks.
    * On the left hand side there are two cells designed for a maximum of ten prisoners.
District 6
  • * District Six was a multi working-class area just off the centre of Cape Town,
    * It was named the sixth district of Cape Town in 1867. Originally established as a community of freed slaves, merchants, artisans, labourers and immigrants.
    * District Six, was an impoverished but lively community of 55 000, predominantly coloured people.
Green market square
  • * Church Square was built in the early 18th century when plots owned by residents were cut back to make way for a public square.
    * The church's graveyard was situated between the church and the Slave Lodge.
    * The Square took its name from the Dutch Reformed Church.
